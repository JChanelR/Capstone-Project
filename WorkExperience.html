All Season end of life Carer

At my brief time at All Seasons, I cared for people in their own homes who were at their end of life, including those paralysed due to strokes, had double amputations, or had severe dementia. I had to aid those who were unable to complete tasks such as feeding themselves, bathing, and dressing/undressing whilst keeping the dignity of the person. Each day would have a very tight schedule, and I had specific training such as using specialised equipment to get someone in and out of a chair or bed who is paralysed or amputated. My day included getting people in and out of bed for the day, bathing them, cleaning soiled beds, dealing with confused and sometimes aggressive client’s with dementia whilst keeping calm, washing soiled underwear for those who were paralysed, ensuring clients take the correct medication, and remembering each clients preferences on how their day is handled. This meant I had to build good relationships with each client, have a strong stomach for soiled bedding/clothing, and keep a level head when a client had passed.

Duties included:

  • Regularly checking out of dates of stock and pouched instruments and replacing and replenishing when necessary.

  • Record keeping on the computer system, including updating patient’s records, creating new patients’ files, updating appointments, and making referrals to other dental professionals (orthodontics, specialists, etc.)

This job also required me to have specific vaccinations. Evidence available upon request.
